## Decisions Made This Session

### D-038: GP Branding folder structure established as single source of truth
**Decision:** Each GP has a dedicated subfolder under `MSIFS/GP-Branding/` containing their profile.md as the authoritative record of all branding research and assets.
**Rationale:** Prevents fragmentation across Notion, docs, and chat history. One place per GP for all bio versions, DISC profiles, research, LinkedIn recommendations, and asset status.

### D-039: Three-tier bio format confirmed as standard (80/175/350 words)
**Decision:** All GP bios are produced in three versions: Short (80 words), Medium (175 words), Long (350 words).
**Rationale:** Confirmed from April 5, 2026 session standard (D-037). Different LP and partner contexts require different lengths — short for digital/signatures, medium for decks, long for formal outreach and DDQ.

### D-040: Partnership-value bio frame adopted
**Decision:** In addition to standard LP-facing bios, each GP has a "Partnership Edition" bio set framed around what they contribute to the other partners specifically.
**Rationale:** Useful for internal alignment, advisory board outreach, and LP due diligence conversations where team cohesion and complementarity are evaluated.

### D-041: Hassan's 6 years in Kazakhstan documented and incorporated into all branding
**Decision:** Hassan's six years living and working in Kazakhstan are now a primary branding element, positioned alongside his East Africa operational base.
**Rationale:** Makes Hassan the only GP with lived, on-the-ground experience in both target regions — a differentiator no credential replicates. Confirmed by Hassan directly during session.

### D-042: Bio language standard — no comparative framing, no AI Memory Brain operational references
**Decision:** GP bios must not contain language that diminishes other partners through comparison ("only partner who," "no other partner can"). AI Memory Brain operational use should not be mentioned in external-facing partner bios.
**Rationale:** Partnership bios should celebrate each individual on their own terms. Comparative language creates hierarchy, not cohesion. AI Memory Brain is an internal operational tool; referencing it in bios is not appropriate for LP or partner audiences.

## Key Learnings

### L-024: GP profiles from April 5 session contained significant data not yet in branding files
**Learning:** The April 5 session created ACS contact profiles (`contacts/Alex.md`, `contacts/Hassan.md`, `contacts/Aibex.md`) and an Alex Bio Brief (`contacts/Alex-Bio-Brief.md`) that contained critical confirmed data — Alex's MS degree field (Quantitative Economics), CERAWeek NextGen Cohort '26, Poets & Quants nomination, Susilo Institute competition, Liquidstar grant scope — none of which had been incorporated into the GP-Branding profile files.
**Fix:** Always check `contacts/` and `decisions-learnings/` from prior sessions before writing new profiles. The April 5 files were the primary source that should have been read first.

### L-025: "Partnership-value bio" is a distinct genre from LP-facing bio
**Learning:** A bio written to explain why a person is valuable to their co-investors requires a fundamentally different frame than a bio written for LPs. The LP bio establishes external credibility; the partnership bio explains internal complementarity. Both are needed.
**Fix:** Produce both versions for each GP. The LP bio goes in the deck; the partnership bio goes in internal materials, advisory board outreach packets, and GP agreement supporting documents.

### L-026: Hassan's Kazakhstan chapter is a primary differentiator, not a footnote
**Learning:** Six years of lived experience in Kazakhstan — one of the fund's two target regions — was initially undocumented in the branding materials despite being highly relevant. It positions Hassan as the only GP with dual-region lived experience.
**Fix:** All future branding work on Hassan should open with this fact. His LinkedIn headline should surface it prominently.
