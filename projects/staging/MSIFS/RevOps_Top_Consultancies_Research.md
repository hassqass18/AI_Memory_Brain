## What is Revenue Operations (RevOps)?

Revenue Operations (RevOps) is the strategic alignment of Sales, Marketing, and Customer Success under a single operational framework designed to drive predictable, scalable, and efficient revenue growth. Rather than operating in silos, RevOps teams share data, tooling, processes, and accountability — creating a unified "revenue machine" across the full customer lifecycle.

The core value proposition of RevOps:
- Eliminates handoff friction between departments
- Establishes a Single Source of Truth (SSoT) for revenue data
- Creates shared definitions for leads, pipeline stages, and success metrics
- Enforces SLAs across the GTM (go-to-market) engine
- Enables data-driven forecasting and decision-making

### 1. Winning by Design
**Headquarters:** Global (distributed) | **Founded:** 2013 | **Founder:** Jacco van der Kooij

**Overview:**
Winning by Design (WbD) is widely regarded as the gold standard in RevOps consulting and training. They apply a scientific, first-principles approach to recurring revenue, treating revenue generation as an engineered system with predictable inputs and repeatable outputs. Their client roster spans hundreds of SaaS companies globally, including major enterprise clients.

**Core Framework: Revenue Architecture**
Revenue Architecture is WbD's foundational methodology — a comprehensive playbook for designing and operating recurring revenue systems that are efficient, scalable, and durable. It is grounded in three pillars:
1. **First Principles** — questioning assumptions and building processes from the ground up
2. **Models & Data** — using standardized models to make informed, data-driven decisions
3. **Systems & Process Design** — scaling recurring revenue using six foundational operational models

The framework is documented in the *Revenue Architecture* textbook (published 2024), which includes 250+ diagrams, tables, and blueprints for GTM leaders.

**Signature Model: The Bow Tie**
The Bow Tie Model replaces the traditional linear sales funnel. It maps the *entire* customer lifecycle — including post-sale adoption and expansion — which the conventional funnel ignores. The Bow Tie covers:
- Left side (Acquisition): Lead generation → Lead development → Sales
- Center: Contract / Close
- Right side (Retention & Growth): Onboarding → Adoption → Expansion → Advocacy

This model reflects the reality that in recurring revenue businesses, growth comes just as much from retaining and expanding existing customers as it does from acquiring new ones.

**Signature Methodology: SPICED**
SPICED is WbD's qualification and discovery framework applied uniformly across the entire customer journey. It stands for:
- **S — Situation:** The prospect's current context, team, and environment
- **P — Pain:** The specific problem or challenge they face
- **I — Impact:** The measurable business impact of solving (or not solving) that problem
- **C — Critical Event:** The deadline, trigger, or event that creates urgency
- **D — Decision:** How they make decisions, who is involved, and what criteria they use

SPICED is designed to create a common language across Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success — so every team member, at every stage of the Bow Tie, is diagnosing the same dimensions of the customer relationship.

**Key Services:**
- GTM and Revenue Architecture design
- Executive and team training programs
- Impact Sprints (rapid engagements to solve specific revenue problems)
- Sales playbook and process design
- RevOps operating model setup

**Pricing:** Training ranges from $1,500–$2,500 per person depending on track.

## Core RevOps Processes (Best Practices Across Top Firms)

Regardless of firm or methodology, the top RevOps consultancies consistently implement the following foundational processes:

### 1. Revenue Operations Audit
Every engagement begins with a structured audit of the current state:
- CRM data quality and architecture review
- Lead lifecycle and funnel stage definitions
- Handoff SLA analysis (marketing → sales → customer success)
- Tech stack inventory and integration health
- Reporting and attribution gap analysis

### 2. Single Source of Truth (SSoT) Architecture
Building a unified data layer where all revenue data flows into one reliable, consistent source. This includes:
- CRM as the system of record
- Clear data ownership and governance policies
- Integration architecture to prevent duplication
- Regular data hygiene programs

### 3. Lead Lifecycle Management
Defining and enforcing the lifecycle stages a prospect passes through from first touch to closed-won and beyond:
- Shared MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead) and SQL (Sales Qualified Lead) definitions
- Handoff SLAs with clear ownership transfers
- Automatic lead routing logic
- Re-engagement workflows for recycled leads

### 4. Pipeline Management & Forecasting
Ensuring pipeline is clean, accurate, and predictable:
- Stage-by-stage entry and exit criteria
- Opportunity hygiene standards
- Forecast categories and commit/upside frameworks
- Automated pipeline health scoring

### 5. Data Governance
Gartner research shows 60% of companies fail to build an end-to-end revenue process without proper governance. Best-in-class RevOps teams establish:
- Written data governance charters
- Field-level ownership (who is responsible for populating each CRM field)
- Validation rules and required fields at key stages
- Regular data audits and cleanup cadences

### 6. Cross-Functional SLAs
Pipeline reliability breaks down when handoffs rely on good intentions rather than enforceable rules. Top RevOps consultancies build:
- Marketing → Sales SLAs (e.g., "Sales follows up on all MQLs within 2 business hours")
- Sales → CS SLAs (e.g., "Customer Success receives handoff package before contract signature")
- Response time standards and escalation paths

### 7. Reporting, Analytics & Attribution
Building dashboards and models that give leadership teams full-funnel visibility:
- Multi-touch attribution (first touch, last touch, linear, U-shaped, W-shaped)
- Stage-to-stage conversion rates
- Win rate by segment, source, and rep
- Customer health scores and expansion signals
- Forecasting accuracy tracking over time

### 8. RevOps Technology Stack
Common tools used by top RevOps teams:
- **CRM:** Salesforce, HubSpot
- **Marketing Automation:** Marketo, HubSpot, Pardot
- **Revenue Intelligence:** Gong, Chorus, Clari
- **Data Enrichment:** ZoomInfo, Clearbit, Apollo.io
- **Business Intelligence:** Looker, Tableau, Metabase
- **Sales Engagement:** Outreach, SalesLoft
- **CPQ / Quote-to-Cash:** Salesforce CPQ, DealHub

## Implications for Provecta Group

Based on this research, the following considerations are most relevant for building or refining a world-class RevOps function internally:

1. **Adopt the Bow Tie mindset** — Stop measuring success at Closed Won. Track the full customer lifecycle including onboarding, adoption, and expansion. This is where recurring revenue is won or lost.

2. **Implement SPICED as a shared language** — A uniform qualification framework across all client-facing teams (BD, Sales, Account Management) dramatically improves handoff quality and deal velocity.

3. **Build toward a RevOps Maturity Model** — Use a structured maturity model (like RevPartners') to honestly assess current operational state and create a phased roadmap for improvement.

4. **Prioritize data governance early** — Clean data is the foundation of all RevOps capabilities. Establish ownership, validation rules, and hygiene cadences before investing in analytics or automation.

5. **Define and enforce SLAs** — Formalize the handoff rules between each department with documented SLAs. This is the single highest-leverage process change most companies can make.

6. **Invest in attribution before tools** — Before adding more software, build the attribution model that makes it possible to understand which activities drive revenue. This informs all subsequent technology investment decisions.
