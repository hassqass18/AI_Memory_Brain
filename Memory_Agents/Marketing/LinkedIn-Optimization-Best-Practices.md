## 1. The Algorithm Fundamentals (2026)

LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes in this order:
1. **Profile completeness** — Only "All-Star" (100% complete) profiles get surfaced. Incomplete profiles are buried.
2. **Recent activity** — Profiles and posts from active users are favored. Dormant accounts lose ranking fast.
3. **Keyword relevance** — The algorithm matches searchers to profiles based on Headline, About, Experience keywords.
4. **Visual quality** — Profiles with professional photos get **14x more views**. Banners signal brand credibility.
5. **Engagement velocity** — Posts that get early engagement (first 60–90 minutes) are amplified. Slow starters die.

**All-Star Profile Requirements:** Photo, Background image, Headline, About (2,000 chars), Current position, Education, Skills (5+), 500+ connections.

## 2. Headline — The Most Critical SEO Element

### Rules
- **220-character limit** — use every character strategically
- Visible everywhere: search results, DMs, comment threads, connection requests
- **Never just a job title** — that's wasted real estate
- Must answer: *Who do I help? With what? What outcome do they get?*

### High-Converting Formula
```
[What you do] | [Who you help] | [Outcome you deliver] | [Credibility signal]
```

### Examples (10K+ follower profiles)
| Creator | Headline Style |
|--------|---------------|
| Justin Welsh | "I help solopreneurs build businesses they love. 550K+ followers." |
| Lara Acosta | "Helping B2B founders grow on LinkedIn | 250K+ creators inspired" |
| Matt Barker | "I write LinkedIn content for B2B founders | 50M+ impressions generated" |
| Alex Hormozi | Business name + outcome metric |

### What to AVOID
- Generic titles: "Director of Sales and Business Development" (employee framing)
- Vague claims: "AI Thought Leader" (unspecific, unsearchable)
- Buzzwords: "Passionate about..." / "Innovative professional..."
- "Seeking new opportunities" in headline (fatal for founder positioning)

## 3. Profile Photo & Banner

### Profile Photo
- Professional, high-resolution headshot
- **Face should fill 60% of the frame**
- Solid or simple background preferred
- Smile or confident expression — approachable but credible
- 400x400px minimum recommended

### Banner (Background Photo)
- Size: **1584 x 396px** — most underutilized profile real estate
- Should communicate your **single core offer or identity** instantly
- High-converting banners include:
  - Your tagline / value proposition
  - A CTA ("DM me for X" / "Download free guide")
  - Product/company branding
  - Proof point ("500K+ professionals reached")
- **Avoid:** personal hobby imagery, stock photos, blank default

## 4. About Section — Your Conversion Copy

### Structure (2,000 characters)
The first **265 characters** show before "See More" — this is your hook. It must stop the scroll.

**Proven structure:**
```
LINE 1 (Hook): Bold problem statement or provocative claim
LINE 2: Who you are and what you've done
LINE 3: Who you specifically help and how
LINE 4-6: Proof points / outcomes / methodology
LINE 7: Soft CTA (DM, link, download)
```

### What Kills the About Section
- Starting with "I am a dynamic professional..." (AI-sounding, ignored)
- Generic filler: "I help businesses grow" (zero specificity)
- Wall of text with no line breaks
- No CTA at the end
- Missing keywords your ICP searches for

## 5. Creator Mode

**Enable if:** You post weekly and want to grow followers (not just connections).

### What Creator Mode Does
- Replaces "Connect" with **"Follow"** (unlocks mass following without connection limits)
- Adds **content topics/hashtags** to profile (signals authority in your niche)
- Unlocks **LinkedIn Newsletter** feature
- Provides enhanced **analytics** on post performance
- Features your content in profile header
- Adds a **Link in Bio** button

### Creator Mode vs. Standard Mode
| Feature | Standard | Creator Mode |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Primary CTA | Connect | Follow |
| Hashtag topics | No | Yes (5 topics) |
| Newsletter | No | Yes |
| Link in Bio | No | Yes |
| Profile layout | Connections first | Content first |

## 6. Featured Section — The Revenue Shelf

The Featured section is prime conversion real estate. Most profiles leave it empty.

**Best-practice Featured items (in priority order):**
1. **Lead magnet / freebie** — your free resource (Starter Kit, checklist, guide)
2. **Booking link** — "Book a Discovery Call" linked directly
3. **Top performing post** — your most viral, authority-building post
4. **Product / offer page** — link to your sales page
5. **Case study or proof post** — a result you delivered

**Limit to 3–4 items.** More dilutes attention.

## 7. Experience Section — Founder Positioning

### For Solo Founders / Company of One
The goal is to appear like an operator with infrastructure, not a freelancer.

**How to frame solo operations professionally:**
- Use your company name as employer (Provecta Group, not "Self-employed")
- Title: "Founder" or "Managing Director" or "Principal" — never "Freelancer"
- Description: Write it like a firm bio — "Provecta Group is a boutique AI consultancy..."
- Show client outcomes, not task lists
- Avoid phrases like "sole proprietor," "independent contractor," "on my own"

**Positioning your experience for authority:**
- Most recent role should be your current company — front and center
- Previous roles: frame them as **building blocks** toward current expertise
- Remove or minimize roles that contradict your current positioning
- Skills on each role should map to your current ICP's keywords

## 9. Content Strategy for 10K+ Followers

### Post Frequency
- **Sweet spot: 4–5 posts/week** for rapid growth
- Minimum to maintain: 2–3 posts/week
- Consistency > virality — algorithm rewards regulars

### Post Format Performance (2026 ranking)
1. **Text-only posts with a strong hook** — highest organic reach
2. **Carousel posts (PDFs)** — best for educational content, high saves
3. **Video posts** — highest engagement rate when personal/authentic
4. **Image posts** — moderate, best for proof/data visuals
5. **Article/Newsletter posts** — lowest reach but builds deep authority
6. **Polls** — high engagement, use sparingly

### Hook Formulas That Work
- "X% of [target audience] don't know this..."
- "I made a mistake that cost me $X. Here's what I learned:"
- "Hot take: [Controversial industry opinion]"
- "Nobody talks about [specific problem]. Here's the truth:"
- "Stop doing [common thing]. Do this instead:"
- "I went from [before state] to [after state]. Here's exactly how:"

### Engagement Triggers
- Ask a question at the end of every post
- Reply to every comment within the first hour (boosts algorithmic reach 3x)
- Comment on 10+ posts daily BEFORE posting your own (warms algorithm)
- Use 3–5 relevant hashtags (not 30 — that's 2019 behavior)

### Content Pillar System (for authority building)
Structure content around 5–7 pillars to signal consistent expertise:
- Rotate pillars throughout the week
- Each pillar should map to a buyer awareness stage
- Combine problem-awareness posts with solution posts (2:1 ratio)

## 10. Solo Founder "Company of One" — Positioning Framework

This is for operators who run a boutique firm solo but want to project credibility and scale without misleading anyone.

### The "Autonomous Operator" Positioning
**Goal:** Appear as a serious, established operator with systems and methodology — not a freelancer between jobs.

**Key signals that communicate scale without overstating:**
- Company name (not "self-employed") as employer on Experience
- "Founder" or "Managing Director" as title — implies leadership
- "We" language in company descriptions (company of one can still say "our clients")
- A company LinkedIn Page (even with 1 employee) — adds credibility badge to profile
- Professional company logo on your experience entry
- Featured section showing products, not just posts
- Named methodology or framework ("The AMB System" vs. "my approach")
- Website / landing page linked (even if simple — shows infrastructure)

**Language to avoid (signals freelancer, not founder):**
- "Available for projects"
- "Open to work"
- "I work with clients to..."
- "Freelance [anything]"
- "Contract [anything]"
- "Sole proprietor"

**Language to use (signals founder / operator):**
- "Provecta Group works with..."
- "Our clients achieve..."
- "The system we've built..."
- "Provecta Group has deployed [X] implementations..."
- "Founder of AI Memory Brain"

## 11. Custom URL & Profile Discoverability

- **Customize your LinkedIn URL** to: `linkedin.com/in/hassanwilson` or `linkedin.com/in/andrewilson`
- Clean URL = easier to share, looks more professional in email signatures
- Avoid the default hash URL (e.g., `andre-hassan-wilson-221b461a2`)

## 12. The "Open to Work" Problem

**Never have "Open to Work" active when positioning as a founder or operator.**

Reasons:
- Signals you need income, not that you have a successful business
- Recruiters and ICPs see it — it undermines your authority
- It contradicts "Founder of X" positioning
- Makes your profile appear as an employee seeking a job, not a business owner acquiring clients

**Disable immediately before any GTM campaign.**
