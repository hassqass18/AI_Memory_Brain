**Rationale:** Benchmarked against emerging/specialty fund best practices ($100M–$300M funds typically use 3–5; FIIMF's dual-region complexity justifies 5–7). Each slot fills a specific GP team capability gap.

## Learnings

### L-001: Warm Introductions Dramatically Outperform Cold Outreach
**Learning:** Research confirmed warm introductions achieve 10–34% reply rates vs. 2–10% for cold email. 85% of advisory roles filled through personal connections.
**Application for MSIFS:** Prioritize leveraging existing LP relationships, DFI networks (IFC, AfDB, EBRD), and GP team's East Africa/Central Asia networks before any cold outreach. If cold outreach is necessary, go through a mutual contact for an introduction rather than direct contact.

### L-002: The "Ask for Advice" Framing Consistently Outperforms Transactional Ask
**Learning:** Research across multiple sources confirms that framing the advisory board ask as seeking the candidate's wisdom (not their name or network) dramatically improves response rates and quality of engagement.
**Application for MSIFS:** Initial outreach messages should open with a specific problem or decision where the candidate's expertise is genuinely needed — not with "we'd like you to join our advisory board."

### L-003: Name Recognition ≠ Value Creation — Engagement is the Only Metric
**Learning:** A common mistake across PE/VC advisory boards is recruiting well-known names who provide minimal engagement. When LPs do due diligence, disengaged advisors become a liability rather than an asset.
**Application for MSIFS:** Before extending any advisory offer, have an explicit conversation about actual time commitment, willingness to make specific introductions, and genuine interest in the fund's thesis. Better to have 3 deeply engaged advisors than 7 passive names.
