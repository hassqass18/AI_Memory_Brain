## 4. Contractor Management

### 4.1 Tender Process

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Prepare Invitation to Tender (ITT) document | AI (draft) + Human (approve) |
| 2 | Prequalify contractors — NCA registered, relevant experience, financial standing | Human (PMO) |
| 3 | Issue ITT to shortlisted contractors (minimum 3) | Human (PMO) |
| 4 | Evaluate tenders — price, programme, methodology, team | AI+Human |
| 5 | Appoint contractor and execute contract | Human (Legal + Hassan) |

### 4.2 Contract Structure

- **Contract type:** Fixed-price lump sum preferred (reduces cost overrun risk)
- **Payment structure:** Milestone-based — payment on certification of completed work stage
- **Retention:** 5% during construction, 2.5% released on practical completion, 2.5% released after defects liability period
- **Defects Liability Period:** 12 months post practical completion
- **Dispute resolution:** Mediation → Arbitration (Kenya Arbitration Act)

### 4.3 On-Site Management

| Role | Responsibility | Owner |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Project Manager | Day-to-day construction oversight, programme management | Human (appointed PM firm) |
| Quantity Surveyor (QS) | Cost control, valuations, change orders | Human (appointed QS firm) |
| Architect | Design compliance, RFI responses, snagging | Human (architect) |
| Structural Engineer | Foundation, frame compliance checks | Human (SE) |
| AI | Document management, progress reporting, cost tracking | AI |
| Hassan | Final approval of significant variations >KES 5M | Human |

## 7. Change Management

All changes to the approved design or contract scope must follow this process:

1. Variation request raised (by contractor or client)
2. QS assesses cost impact; PM assesses programme impact
3. If cost impact < KES 1M — PM can approve
4. If cost impact KES 1M–5M — Hassan approval required
5. If cost impact > KES 5M — Hassan approval + logged in Bugs.md as a formal change
6. All approved variations logged in `Product_Development/Releases/Bugs.md`

## 8. Unit Handover Procedure

### 8.1 Pre-Handover

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | NCA Completion Certificate obtained | Human (Hassan / PM) |
| 2 | Snagging list prepared for each unit | AI+Human (architect + PM) |
| 3 | Snagging items rectified by contractor | Human (Contractor) |
| 4 | Final QS sign-off on unit standard | Human (QS) |
| 5 | Title deeds / transfer documents prepared | Human (Legal) |
| 6 | Service charge account established | Human (Property Manager) |

### 8.2 Handover Day

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Buyer invited to unit inspection | AI (coordinate booking) + Human (PM on site) |
| 2 | Buyer signs Unit Acceptance Form | Human (Buyer + PM) |
| 3 | Keys, access cards, fobs handed over | Human (PM) |
| 4 | Welcome Pack provided | AI (prepare) + Human (deliver) |
| 5 | CRM updated — unit status → Handed Over | AI |
| 6 | Final payment received (balance) | Human (Finance) |

### 8.3 Welcome Pack Contents (Per Unit)

- Unit keys (2 sets) + fob/access card
- Building handbook — rules, facilities, service charges
- Emergency contacts — property manager, security, maintenance
- Defects reporting procedure (12-month defects liability)
- Amenity usage guide (pool, gym, rooftop)
- Service charge schedule
