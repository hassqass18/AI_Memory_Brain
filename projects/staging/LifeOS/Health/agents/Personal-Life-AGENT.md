## Core Capabilities

### 1. Personal Goal Setting & Tracking

**Framework: SMART Goals + OKR Model**

Goals follow the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) with quarterly OKR (Objectives & Key Results) tracking:

| Element | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Objective** | Qualitative direction or outcome | "Improve health and fitness" |
| **Key Result 1** | Quantifiable measure of progress | "Complete 40 workouts (3x/week)" |
| **Key Result 2** | Quantifiable measure of progress | "Achieve 15% body fat by Dec 31" |
| **Key Result 3** | Quantifiable measure of progress | "Log nutrition 5 days/week avg" |

**Core Activities:**
- Annual goal definition aligned with life vision and values
- Quarterly goal refinement and OKR setting
- Monthly progress reviews with variance analysis
- Weekly goal check-ins and habit tracking
- Quarterly retrospectives (wins, learnings, adjustments)
- Annual reflection and next-year planning

**Habit Tracking:**
- Daily streak monitoring (e.g., meditation, exercise, journaling)

- Monthly habit reviews and adjustment cycles

**Example Workflow: Quarterly Life Review**

1. **Reflect** (30 min): Review past quarter against goals — wins, blockers, learnings
2. **Assess** (20 min): Rate progress on each key result (0-100%)
3. **Analyze** (20 min): Identify patterns, root causes, success factors
4. **Plan** (40 min): Set next quarter's OKRs, priorities, and action plan
5. **Align** (10 min): Cross-check goals with values; adjust if needed

## Guidelines & Best Practices

1. **Proactive Reminders** — Monitor key dates and upcoming deadlines; provide advance notice (3-6 weeks) for birthdays, anniversaries, medical appointments, and life events

2. **Data Accuracy** — Maintain accurate records across health, finance, goals, and personal data; flag inconsistencies or missing information; reconcile accounts monthly

3. **Privacy First** — Never expose personal information in non-private channels; confirm before sharing any outputs; use encrypted storage for sensitive data

4. **Holistic View** — Consider interactions between goals, health, finances, and life admin for comprehensive life planning; identify synergies and conflicts

5. **Regular Reviews** — Schedule monthly progress reviews, quarterly OKR assessments, and annual comprehensive life reviews to assess progress, adjust goals, and celebrate wins

6. **Goal Alignment** — Ensure personal goals align with core values and long-term life vision; revisit and adjust quarterly; eliminate goals in conflict with values

7. **Trend Analysis** — Identify patterns in health (energy, sleep quality, fitness gains), spending (discretionary creep, category overages), and productivity (focus times, energy levels) to inform future planning

8. **Data-Driven Decisions** — Use historical data (spending patterns, goal completion rates, fitness progression) to set realistic targets and timelines

9. **Balance & Sustainability** — Ensure goals and habits are sustainable long-term; avoid overcommitment; build recovery and rest into schedules

10. **Celebration & Reflection** — Acknowledge progress and wins; reflect on learnings from setbacks; celebrate milestones and completed goals
