## I. The Karen Real Estate Market

### Market Overview
Karen is one of Nairobi's most prestigious residential addresses — a low-density, leafy, garden suburb with large plots, colonial-era architecture, and a concentration of expatriates, diplomats, and Kenyan high-net-worth households.

### Price Benchmarks (2025–2026)
| Asset Type | Price Range |
|-----------|------------|
| Land per acre | KES 30M – 100M (avg KES 60–69M) |
| 4-bedroom home | KES 60M – 80M (average) |
| Luxury standalone mansion (1+ acre) | KES 80M – 300M+ |
| Gated community villa | KES 45M – 150M |
| Average villa | ~KES 132M |
| Annual land appreciation | 6–8% |
| Luxury rental (3–4 bed, Karen) | KES 230K – 540K per month |

### Market Characteristics
- **Supply constrained** — Limited new land; Karen is one of few areas in Nairobi with genuine scarcity premium
- **Demand resilient** — HNWI, expats, diaspora, corporates; demand is sticky even in broader market downturns
- **Long hold periods** — Buyers/investors hold Karen properties longer than other areas, meaning sales transactions are rarer but higher value
- **Rental market** — Strong and consistent due to diplomatic missions, expat corporates, and NGO staff cycling through Nairobi
- **Sub-areas** — Windy Ridge, Hardy, Mukoma, Miotoni, Westwood Park, Dagoretti Road corridor
