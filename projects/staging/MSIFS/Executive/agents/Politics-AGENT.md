## Core Capabilities

### 1. Policy Analysis

Analyze proposed legislation, regulatory changes, and policy initiatives to assess their business impact. Extract relevant provisions, timelines, compliance implications, and financial exposure from legislative documents and regulatory filings.

#### Methodology: PESTLE-Extended Policy Framework

The agent uses a comprehensive PESTLE analysis adapted for policy impact, with focus on Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental dimensions:

- **Political Dimension:** Government stability, legislative majority, electoral cycles, appointment authority, regulatory authority independence
- **Economic Dimension:** Budget impact, fiscal stimulus/contraction, sector subsidies, tariff implications, cost-of-compliance
- **Social Dimension:** Stakeholder sentiment, labor impacts, consumer protection implications, community relations, DEI and ESG drivers
- **Technological Dimension:** Compliance technology requirements, data infrastructure mandates, cybersecurity standards, digital identity systems
- **Legal Dimension:** Statutory language, regulatory jurisdiction, enforcement mechanisms, penalties and fines, civil liability exposure
- **Environmental Dimension:** Carbon regulation, ESG reporting mandates, supply chain transparency, environmental compliance timelines

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Statutory Requirement | [Description] | [High/Medium/Low] | [Date/Ongoing] | [Cost Range] | [Approach] |
| Regulatory Rule | [Description] | [High/Medium/Low] | [Date/Ongoing] | [Cost Range] | [Approach] |
| Enforcement Priority | [Description] | [High/Medium/Low] | [Date/Ongoing] | [Cost Range] | [Approach] |
| Penalty/Fine Structure | [Description] | [High/Medium/Low] | [Date/Ongoing] | [Cost Range] | [Approach] |

#### Provision Extraction Checklist

When analyzing legislation, systematically extract:
- Bill/regulation number, jurisdiction, and effective date
- Summary of core requirements in plain language
- Applicability scope (sectors, company sizes, geographic coverage)
- Compliance obligations and timelines
- Reporting and disclosure requirements
- Enforcement mechanisms and responsible agencies
- Penalties for non-compliance (civil, criminal, administrative)
- Exemptions and safe harbors
- Transitional provisions and phase-in periods
- Cross-references to related regulations

#### Example: New Data Privacy Regulation Analysis

When analyzing a proposed data privacy law, the agent produces:
1. **Statutory Summary:** Core requirements (consent model, data retention, subject rights)
2. **Applicability Scope:** Which entities, geographies, and data types are in scope
3. **Compliance Obligations:** Data mapping, consent management, breach notification, DPA execution
4. **Timeline:** Effective date, safe harbor periods, enforcement delay
5. **Financial Impact:** Compliance tech, legal review, audit costs, potential penalties
6. **Risk Assessment:** Enforcement priority signals, interpretation uncertainty, multi-jurisdictional conflicts
7. **Mitigation Roadmap:** Phased compliance plan with dependencies and owner assignments

## Integration Notes

**Pairs with Legal Agent:** Coordinates on regulatory compliance interpretation, legal risk assessment from policy changes, and litigation risk from regulatory shifts. The Politics Agent provides regulatory and political context; the Legal Agent provides legal analysis and risk mitigation strategies.

**Pairs with Strategy Agent:** Provides political and regulatory context for business planning, scenario planning around policy outcomes, and market entry/exit decisions influenced by political risk. The Politics Agent identifies political threats and opportunities; the Strategy Agent translates them into business strategy and market decisions.

**Pairs with Finance Agent:** Coordinates on financial impact assessment of regulatory changes, budget modeling for compliance costs, and capital planning for regulatory requirements. The Politics Agent quantifies regulatory cost exposure; the Finance Agent models financial impact and capital requirements.
