## 3. Indicative Floor-by-Floor Layout

*Distribution below is indicative pending architect's final drawings. Aim: concentrate 3BR on higher floors (premium views, premium pricing), 2BR on mid floors, 1BR on lower floors.*

### Ground Floor
- Entrance lobby
- Security post / guard station
- Mailboxes / concierge area
- Parking entrance and circulation
- Stairwell and lift cores
- Access to pool, gym, and communal areas

---

### Floors 1–3 (Lower Residential)
- Indicative mix: primarily 2BR and 1BR
- ~3× 1BR, ~6× 2BR per floor
- Floors 1–3: 9 units × 3 floors = 27 units
- Elevator, fire escape, service riser access

---

### Floors 4–7 (Mid Residential)
- Indicative mix: 2BR and 3BR
- ~3× 2BR, ~6× 3BR per floor (subject to architectural layout)
- Floors 4–7: 9 units × 4 floors = 36 units

---

### Floors 8–11 (Upper Residential — Premium)
- Indicative mix: predominantly 3BR
- ~9× 3BR per floor or mix of 2BR/3BR depending on floor plate
- Higher floors command view premium — pricing may be stratified on completion
- Floors 8–11: 9 units × 4 floors = 36 units (includes some 1BR/2BR to reach totals)
- Floor 11: May include penthouse or premium 3BR configurations

## 4. Unit Numbering Convention (Proposed)

Format: `[Floor][Type][Number]`

Examples:
- `G01` = Ground floor feature 1
- `101` = Floor 1, Unit 1
- `204` = Floor 2, Unit 4
- `1101` = Floor 11, Unit 1

*Final numbering convention to be confirmed by architect and used consistently across all sales, legal, and operational documents.*
