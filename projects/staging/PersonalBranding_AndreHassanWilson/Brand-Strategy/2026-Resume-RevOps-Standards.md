## Part 1 — 2026 Resume Standards

### Format & Length
- **Two pages** is the standard for senior/executive professionals (7+ years experience). One page signals junior.
- **Hybrid/combination format** dominates: strong skills section above the fold, followed by reverse-chronological experience.
- **No sidebars, text boxes, tables, or graphics** — ATS systems reject them. 75% of resumes are screened out by ATS before a human sees them.
- Fonts: Arial, Calibri, or Roboto at 10–12pt. Margins: 0.5–1 inch. Line spacing: 1.0–1.15.
- Replace long bullet lists with grouped achievements under strategic themes (e.g., Growth, Efficiency, Scale).

### Required Sections (in order)
1. **Headline / Professional Title** — reflects current positioning, not past job title
2. **Executive Summary** (3–4 lines) — reads like a business case; answers "how does this person create enterprise-level results?"
3. **Core Competencies / Skills** — hard skills + tools separated into clean columns (no diamond separators)
4. **Reverse-Chronological Experience** — 2–3 quantified impact bullets per role using formula below
5. **Certifications & Tools** — standalone section; critical for ATS keyword matching in RevOps
6. **Education**

### Bullet Formula (2026 Standard)
> **Action verb + What you did + How you did it + Result (with number)**

Example: *"Redesigned outbound pipeline architecture using HubSpot Sequences and custom lead scoring, reducing sales cycle by 22% and increasing MQL-to-SQL conversion from 18% to 34%."*

Candidates with metrics see **40% higher response rates.**

### What Gets You Rejected in 2026
- Generic "AI-written" language — 62% of employers reject these
- No quantified results — subjective language without numbers is filtered out
- Missing AI/automation literacy — now a **baseline leadership competency**, not a bonus
- Objective statements (obsolete — replace with executive summary)
- "References available upon request" (obsolete — remove)
- Passive language: "Responsible for", "Duties included", "Helped with"

### Executive Resume Trends (Senior Level)
- Resumes now read like a **business case**, not a job description
- Achievements grouped under **strategic themes**: Growth | Efficiency | Scale | Transformation
- AI capability must be demonstrated with **specific use cases and measurable outcomes**
- Clean, purposeful design — no decorative elements that confuse ATS
- LinkedIn URL should match the resume's positioning precisely

## Part 3 — RevOps Consultant Positioning (2026)

### What Clients Pay Premium For
1. **Systems thinkers** — designing workflows that scale across teams, not just departments
2. **AI integrators** — practitioners who have embedded AI into real operations with measurable outcomes
3. **Data-fluent operators** — can interpret, validate, and act on revenue data without a data team
4. **Cross-functional translators** — bridge Sales, Marketing, Finance, and CS without friction
5. **Change management leaders** — can drive adoption of new systems across resistant organizations

### Consultant vs. Operator Distinction
A RevOps *Consultant* is distinguished from a RevOps *Manager* by:
- Diagnosing problems across multiple clients/industries
- Designing scalable frameworks (not just executing within one)
- Quantifying ROI of operational changes
- Advising C-suite on revenue architecture decisions
