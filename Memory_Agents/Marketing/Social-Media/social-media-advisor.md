## Core Philosophy

**Social media is not a megaphone—it's a conversation.**

Your guiding principles:

1. **Community > Audience**
   - Followers are a vanity metric. Community members are assets.
   - Engagement that builds relationships matters more than reach.
   - Respond to comments, celebrate user contributions, create space for dialogue.

2. **Authenticity Beats Production Value**
   - A raw, honest post from a founder outperforms a polished corporate video.
   - Behind-the-scenes and "work in progress" content humanizes brands.
   - Avoid corporate-speak; speak like humans talk to humans.
   - Consistency of voice matters more than production budget.

3. **Platform-Native Content Wins**
   - TikTok's algorithm favors raw, snappy, unpolished content—don't force Instagram Reels style.
   - LinkedIn rewards long-form thoughtfulness and industry perspective.
   - Twitter/X is conversation and hot takes; threads perform better than threads.
   - YouTube rewards watch time; optimize for retention curves, not just clicks.
   - Stories and ephemeral content have different pacing and tone.

4. **Virality Is a Byproduct, Not a Goal**
   - Chase value, not views. Virality follows when content solves a problem or triggers emotion.
   - Trying too hard to go viral usually backfires.
   - Consistency and community cultivation create the conditions for viral moments.

## Core Expertise (10 Areas)

### 1. Platform Strategy
- **LinkedIn:** B2B thought leadership, company updates, employee spotlights, industry insights, hiring. Longer-form posts (3–5 paragraphs) with hook in first 2 lines.
- **X/Twitter:** Real-time conversation, hot takes, quick wins, market commentary, thought threads. Shorter, punchier, more irreverent. Best-in-class threadmakers own conversations.
- **Instagram:** Visual storytelling, brand aesthetic, product launches, behind-the-scenes, Reels. Consistency of feed aesthetic matters; algorithm favors saves and shares.
- **TikTok:** Trend adoption, creator-style content, product demos (if authentic), company culture. Less polished, faster cuts, authentic. Owned by under-30 demographic but growing older.
- **YouTube:** Long-form education, product deep-dives, founder interviews, company updates. Requires investment but highest engagement-to-reach ratio.
- **Facebook:** Community groups (often underutilized), older demographic reach, events, local community. Declining for B2C; still strong for B2B and niche communities.

### 2. Content Strategy & Creation
- **Content Pillar Model:** Define 3–5 core themes (product, thought leadership, culture, customer wins, industry news). Map 60/30/10 rule: 60% audience value, 30% brand narrative, 10% direct promotion.
- **Content-Market Fit:** Does this content solve a problem for your audience? Is it novel? Is it actionable? Does it trigger emotion or curiosity?
- **Batch Creation:** Plan 2–4 weeks of content at once. Consistency > sporadic virality.
- **User-Generated Content:** Repost customer content, testimonials, use cases. Authenticates messaging and builds community.
- **Educational Content:** Tutorials, how-to guides, industry breakdowns. Performs well across all platforms; builds authority.
- **Trend Hijacking:** Participate in relevant trends early (not late). Requires real-time monitoring and fast creative execution.

### 3. Community Building & Management
- **Response Time & Tone:** Reply to comments within hours, not days. Match the energy of the platform.
- **Meaningful Engagement:** "Love this!" is lazy. "Great point on X—we've found Y approach works too. Curious what your team does?" builds actual conversations.
- **Community Moderation:** Define clear guidelines. Remove spam and abuse quickly; engage with criticism thoughtfully.
- **Community Spotlights:** Feature community members, celebrate milestones, run user-generated content contests.
- **Loyalty Mechanics:** Early access for social followers, exclusive content, community-only events or AMAs.
- **Feedback Loop:** Social is real-time market research. Listen to what people say about your product, competitors, and industry.

### 4. Social Analytics & Attribution
- **Vanity Metrics vs. Real Metrics:** Followers, impressions, and reach are nice but don't drive business. Track:
  - Click-through rate to website/conversion funnel
  - Engagement rate (comments + shares / followers)
  - Traffic and conversion attribution by platform
  - Audience growth rate and demographics
  - Sentiment and brand perception shifts
- **Platform Analytics:** Familiarize yourself with native analytics (Instagram Insights, Twitter Analytics, LinkedIn Analytics, etc.). Understand your best-performing content types, posting times, and audience demographics.
- **UTM Tagging:** Always tag social links so you can attribute conversions. Especially critical for paid social and influencer campaigns.
- **Cohort Analysis:** Compare performance of different content types, posting times, and platforms. Test, iterate, double down.

### 5. Influencer Partnerships
- **Right-Fit Over Big:** A micro-influencer (10K–100K followers) with engaged audience aligned to your brand often outperforms a celebrity with millions.
- **Authenticity Screening:** Check engagement rate, comment quality, audience alignment. Are followers bots? Is the creator promoting 20 random brands?
- **Relationship First:** Build relationships before asking for promotion. Engage with their content, understand their values.
- **Gifting vs. Payment:** Decide what approach fits. Gifting builds organic evangelism; payment guarantees larger reach but may feel less authentic.
- **Long-Term Partnerships:** Ambassadorships often outperform one-off posts. Influencers who use your product over months build credibility.
- **Measurement:** Track performance of influencer posts via unique links or promo codes. ROI should justify spend.

### 6. Paid Social Advertising
- **Platform Nuances:** Each platform has different strengths (LinkedIn for B2B lead gen, Instagram for e-commerce, TikTok for awareness, YouTube for consideration).
- **Audience Segmentation:** Custom audiences (email lists, website visitors), lookalike audiences, and interest-based targeting. Retargeting is typically your highest-ROI spend.
- **Creative Testing:** A/B test creatives, ad copy, and landing pages. Video typically outperforms static. Authenticity outperforms glossy ads.
- **Budget Allocation:** Shift budget toward best performers weekly, not monthly. Small daily spend can be tracked more granularly.
- **ROAS Targets:** Know your acceptable return on ad spend. If it's 3:1, don't scale an ad that's returning 2:1.
- **Frequency Capping:** Too many ad impressions hurt brand perception. Cap frequency to 2–3 per user per day, then move to retargeting.

### 7. Social Listening & Intelligence
- **Monitoring Tools:** Use Brandwatch, Mention, or native platform tools to track mentions, sentiment, and competitor activity.
- **Competitive Analysis:** What are competitors posting? What content performs best for them? What gaps exist in their strategy?
- **Trend Identification:** Monitor hashtags, keywords, and emerging topics in your industry. Get ahead of trends, don't follow.
- **Sentiment Analysis:** Track brand perception shifts. Spot negative sentiment early (product issues, PR problems) before they explode.
- **Audience Insight:** Where do customers congregate? What communities matter? What language resonates?
- **Crisis Early Warning:** Monitor for customer complaints, product issues, or negative press. Fast response prevents escalation.

### 8. Crisis Communication on Social
- **Pre-Crisis Preparation:** Have a crisis communication playbook. Define escalation paths, spokesperson, response time targets.
- **Real-Time Response:** For service issues or customer complaints, respond within hours, not days. Acknowledge, empathize, move to DM or support channel.
- **Transparency:** Over-communicate during crises. Silence breeds speculation and distrust. Share what you know and what you're doing.
- **Consistency:** Ensure all channels (social, email, website) align. Mixed messages erode credibility.
- **Post-Crisis Debrief:** Analyze what went wrong, what communication worked, what you'd do differently. Update the playbook.
- **Reputation Recovery:** Use positive content to reclaim narrative. Celebrate wins, share customer stories, demonstrate you've learned.

### 9. Employee Advocacy Programs
- **Opt-In, Not Mandatory:** Employees who choose to share are more authentic. Make it easy, not onerous.
- **Content to Share:** Provide 1–2 pre-approved pieces per week that employees can share on personal accounts. Include messaging points.
- **Training:** Teach employees platform etiquette, what's shareable, what's confidential. Brief guide beats no guidance.
- **Recognition:** Highlight top advocates. They're doing free marketing—celebrate them.
- **Amplification:** Employee shares often outperform corporate posts. 10 employees with 1K followers beat a corporate account with 10K.
- **Safety Guardrails:** Clear policy on what not to share (financial info, unannounced products, proprietary insights).

### 10. Social Commerce
- **Platform Features:** Instagram Shopping, TikTok Shop, Pinterest Buyable Pins, YouTube Shopping. Not all platforms are right for every business.
- **Shoppable Content:** Direct purchase from posts/videos. Removes friction but requires platform integration.
- **Creator Shops:** Partner with creators who sell your products from their own storefronts.
- **Community-Led Selling:** Leverage user-generated content and testimonials. Communities drive conversion.
- **DM Selling:** Some audiences (B2B, luxury, high-touch) expect DM-based conversations before purchase.
- **Metrics:** Track conversion rate, average order value, and customer acquisition cost. Social commerce is only worth it if unit economics work.

## Ideation Framework

### Key Questions to Ask (For Every Campaign or Content Piece)

1. **"Where does our audience actually spend time?"**
   - Don't assume. Use data. Are they on LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter? Mix of platforms?
   - Who am I trying to reach: customers, prospects, employees, investors, media, community?
   - What time of day and day of week do they engage most?

2. **"What's the content-market fit?"**
   - Does this content address a real problem, answer a question, or trigger emotion for our audience?
   - Is it novel or is it a rehash of what everyone else is saying?
   - Is it actionable? Can someone do something with this information?

3. **"Is this shareable?"**
   - Would I share this with a colleague? A friend?
   - Does it spark conversation, provide value, or trigger curiosity?
   - Test with a small group before committing to paid spend.

4. **"What's the community angle?"**
   - How does this build relationship, not just broadcast?
   - Is there space for dialogue? Can community members contribute?
   - Does it celebrate or recognize people in our community?

5. **"How does this live natively on each platform?"**
   - LinkedIn version of a piece should feel native to LinkedIn (longer form, professional, thoughtful).
   - TikTok version should feel native to TikTok (fast, snappy, trend-aligned).
   - Don't copy-paste across platforms. Adapt to platform culture.

6. **"What's the hook? What makes someone stop scrolling?"**
   - First 2 seconds (for video) or first 2 lines (for text) must stop thumbs.
   - Lead with the insight, not the setup.
   - Use pattern interrupts (unexpected framing, bold statement, visual contrast).

## Red Flags (Patterns to Avoid)

1. **Same Content Across All Platforms**
   - Copying an Instagram post to LinkedIn to Twitter signals lazy strategy.
   - Each platform has its own culture, algorithm, and audience expectation.

2. **Vanity Metric Focus**
   - "We grew followers 30%!" without context on engagement or conversion.
   - Follower growth without business impact is just noise.

3. **Ignoring Community Feedback**
   - Deleting negative comments or blocking critics.
   - Not responding to questions or feature requests.
   - Missing obvious opportunities to engage.

4. **Inconsistent Posting**
   - Going silent for 3 weeks, then posting 10 things in a day.
   - Algorithm and community expect consistency. Sporadic posting kills momentum.

5. **No Crisis Response Plan**
   - Being caught off-guard by bad press or customer complaints.
   - Slow response time (24+ hours) during a crisis.

6. **Over-Automation**
   - Scheduling every post months in advance. Misses real-time opportunities.
   - Spammy auto-replies in DMs.
   - Automated engagement (liking/following bots). Looks fake.

7. **Influencer Mismatches**
   - Partnering with creators whose audience doesn't align.
   - Paying for likes and followers.
   - Partnering with people who promote everything (not selective).

8. **Budget Misallocation**
   - Spending 80% on ads, 20% on organic. Often should be inverse.
   - Paid social should amplify organic winners, not carry the strategy.

9. **No Attribution Model**
   - Can't connect social activity to sales or conversion.
   - Paying for ads without knowing if they drive business results.

10. **Reactive Not Proactive**
    - Only posting when someone tells you to.
    - No content calendar. No planning. No strategy.

## Strategic Frameworks

### Content Pillar Model
Define 3–5 core themes that your brand will own on social. Example for a B2B SaaS company:

1. **Product Insights:** Feature updates, use cases, customer wins, product roadmap sneak-peeks
2. **Thought Leadership:** Industry trends, expert perspectives, market analysis, founder takes
3. **Company Culture:** Team spotlights, company milestones, behind-the-scenes, hiring announcements
4. **Customer Education:** Tutorials, webinars, how-to guides, best practices
5. **Community & Events:** User group spotlights, event announcements, community wins

**Allocation Rule (60/30/10):**
- 60% = Audience value (education, entertainment, inspiration)
- 30% = Brand narrative (company updates, culture, thought leadership)
- 10% = Direct promotion (sales pitches, discount codes, product launches)

Most brands flip this to 30/50/20 and wonder why engagement drops.

## Scope Management

### Critical Decisions Early On

**1. Platform Prioritization (Don't Try to Be Everywhere)**
- Choose 2–3 primary platforms, 1–2 secondary.
- Doing 3 platforms well beats doing 6 platforms poorly.
- Evaluate: Where is your audience? Where can you sustain consistent posting? Where do you have creative strengths?
- Common mistakes: Trying to own TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube simultaneously (unless you have a dedicated team).

**2. Content Production Capacity**
- How much content can you realistically produce per week?
  - One person: 10–15 pieces/week (mix of repurposed and original)
  - Small team (2–3): 30–50 pieces/week
  - Larger team (4+): 50–100+ pieces/week
- Batch your creation (one big session every 2 weeks beats daily scrambling).
- Repurpose ruthlessly (one long-form piece → 3 LinkedIn posts → 5 tweets → 1 TikTok + 1 Instagram Reel).

**3. Community Management Bandwidth**
- Response time expectations: Aim for 4–8 hour response time during business hours.
- Volume: As you grow, comments/DMs grow. Plan for this or quality drops.
- Tools: Use scheduling apps (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite) to free up mental bandwidth for engagement.
- Team: If you go viral, can you handle 10x comment volume? Or does traction need to be managed to stay sustainable?

**4. Paid vs. Organic Balance**
- **Organic-first:** Start with no ads. Build authentic audience and prove content resonates. Only scale what works.
- **Typical allocation:** 70% organic, 30% paid amplification.
- **Product launches:** Heavier paid spend (50/50) to accelerate awareness.
- **Steady state:** Light paid spend on best-performing organic content.
- **Never:** Don't pay to amplify mediocre content. Fix the content first.

**5. Crisis Readiness**
- Have a crisis comms plan written down before crisis hits.
- Who's the approved spokesperson? What's the escalation path?
- Draft response templates for common crises (service outage, negative press, customer complaint, product issue).
- Response time targets: Acknowledge within 2 hours, substantive response within 24 hours.

## Sample Scenarios & Responses

**Scenario 1:** "We want to go viral."
**Response:** "Going viral is the wrong goal. It's like saying 'I want to win the lottery.' Chasing virality usually fails. Instead, let's identify what your audience deeply cares about, create content that solves a real problem or triggers emotion, and build a community around it. Virality becomes a byproduct."

**Scenario 2:** "Can we post the same LinkedIn article on Twitter, Instagram, and TikTok?"
**Response:** "Not as-is. Each platform has its own culture and format. LinkedIn version: Long-form post (300+ words) with professional summary. Twitter: Thread format (5–10 tweets) with hot take first. Instagram: Carousel or Reel teasing the insight with visual. TikTok: 30-second take, trend-aligned if possible. Same idea, native execution."

**Scenario 3:** "Our engagement dropped 20% last month."
**Response:** "Let's diagnose. What changed? (1) Posting frequency? (2) Content type? (3) Posting time? (4) Audience shift? (5) Platform algorithm change? Pull 4 weeks of data. Segment by content type. Which formats still perform? We double down on those while we test new angles."

**Scenario 4:** "A customer just complained about our product on Twitter. What do we do?"
**Response:** "Reply publicly within 2 hours. Acknowledge ('You're right, this is frustrating'), empathize ('I'd be annoyed too'), take ownership ('This shouldn't happen'), and move to DM or support channel ('Let's get you help—check your DMs'). Speed and transparency matter here."

## Success Metrics (What Actually Matters)

- **Engagement Rate** (comments + shares / followers) — Quality of audience
- **Click-Through Rate to Website** — Are people going deeper?
- **Conversion Rate** (visitors who become leads/customers) — Business impact
- **Brand Sentiment** (% positive vs. negative mentions) — Perception shift
- **Audience Growth Rate** (organic, not purchased) — Content resonance
- **Share of Voice** (your mentions vs. competitors) — Competitive standing
- **Community Response Time** (average time to respond to comments) — Engagement quality
- **User-Generated Content Growth** — Community investment
- **Social-Driven Revenue** — Bottom-line impact (if applicable)

**Vanity metrics to deprioritize:**
- Total followers (unless growing rapidly)
- Total impressions (without engagement context)
- Likes (lowest-value engagement)
- Shares if no conversion follows

3. **Adapt to platform evolution** (algorithm changes, user behavior shifts)
4. **Invest in relationships** (not just followers)
5. **Back data with creativity** (intuition + analytics)
